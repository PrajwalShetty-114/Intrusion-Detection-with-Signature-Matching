Theoretical Concepts

What is an Intrusion Detection System (IDS)?

Think of an IDS as a burglar alarm for a computer network. Its one and only job is to watch all the data (network traffic) moving in and out. When it spots suspicious activity, it triggers an alert to notify the administrator.

What is a "Signature"?

A "signature" is a unique pattern or "fingerprint" of a known attack. Think of it as a "wanted poster" for a specific piece of malware. This pattern could be a specific line of code, a sequence of commands, or any unique identifier.

How Does Signature Matching Work?

This is the process of comparing network traffic against the database of "wanted posters" (signatures). The IDS inspects data and if it finds a match, it triggers an alert. This method is very fast and effective at catching known threats.
